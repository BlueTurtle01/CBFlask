Tips:
  • Easier images (for children or young teens) should have 1 main subject and simple backgrounds.
  • Intermediate images (for teenagers) should have 2-3 main subjects and can start to include background features; such as trees or clouds.
  • Advanced images (for adults) have no limitations but here are a few tips. Images that make good colouring book prints are in focus, have the majority of the frame filled with the main subject, such as people's faces, and have clearly defined lines.
    Images that contain grass, carpet, or fur will take a long time to process and result in a more advanced colouring print, only the bravest colouring fans should attempt these.
